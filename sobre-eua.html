What is Global Warming?

Global warming can be defined as the process of average rise in Earth's temperatures over of time. According to most scientific studies and climate panel reports, its occurrence would be being accelerated by human activities, causing atmospheric and ocean level problems, thanks to to melting of polar ice caps. The main body responsible for disseminating data and information on Global Warming is the Panel on Climate Change (IPCC), a body linked to the United Nations (UN). according to IPCC, the 20th century was the warmest in recent times, with an average increase of 0.7ºC in temperatures in the entire terrestrial globe. The estimate, according to the same organ, is that temperatures will continue to rise to the throughout the 21st century if actions to contain the problem are not adopted on a large scale. The IPCC basically works with two scenarios: one optimistic and the other pessimistic. In the first, considering that O human being can reduce the emission of pollutants in the atmosphere and contain deforestation actions, the temperatures would rise by 1°C by 2100. In the second scenario, temperatures could rise by 1.8 up until 4ºC during this same period, which would compromise a good part of human activities. In a high-profile report published in March 2014, the IPCC states that global warming would be very serious and irreversible, causing the elevation of the oceans, agricultural losses, among other countless catastrophes generated by changes in the climate and in the disposition of elements and natural resources.
